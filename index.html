Fundraising Automation · Multi-million pound charitable enterprise managing Gift Aid tracking, retail EPOS systems, and grant applications across multiple locations
Nov 2025 · Build: 2h · Saves: 6d/yr

The fundraiser faced a recurring burden: identifying suitable grant funders, then completing lengthy applications that repeatedly requested identical organisational information. Each application consumed a full working day—researching eligibility criteria across multiple funder databases, adapting project descriptions to different question formats, and re-entering standard details.

I built a system that stores the organisation's core information and uses examples from previous applications to maintain consistent tone. When seeking funding, the fundraiser describes project requirements and the system searches for matching opportunities, then generates draft applications adapted to each funder's specific questions whilst preserving the organisation's established voice.

Applications that previously required eight hours now need ten minutes of review and submission. With six applications annually, this recovers six working days—time now spent building funder relationships rather than processing paperwork.

Production Tracking Automation · Publishing house managing production workflows, editorial tracking, and scripture reference systems
Nov 2025 · Build: 4h · Saves: 13d/yr

The editor couldn't answer basic questions about book status. Information lived scattered across five spreadsheets—editorial progress in one, proofreading stages in another, indexing elsewhere. Finding out whether a title would meet its print deadline required searching multiple files and asking several people.

I built a unified tracker showing every book's stage, responsible person, and deadline in one place. A traffic light system flags titles at risk, and percentage completion compares against target progress. Staff update it manually as work completes, avoiding the complexity of automated integrations whilst maintaining current visibility.

The editor previously spent two hours weekly chasing status information across disconnected systems. That time is eliminated. The system didn't prevent measurable failures—books weren't missing print slots—but it transformed operational chaos into clarity, allowing the editor to focus on editorial decisions rather than detective work.

Sales Data Restructuring · Multi-million pound charitable enterprise managing Gift Aid tracking, retail EPOS systems, and grant applications across multiple locations
Dec 2025 · Build: 20h · Saves: 13d/yr

Management needed comprehensible dashboards to inform stock and pricing decisions. The underlying sales data made this impossible. The spreadsheet contained 43,000 formulas sprawled across multiple sheets, with duplicated data and calculations buried in beige and green cells. Most of the data went ignored because interpretation required forensic effort.

I restructured the entire database, rotating the layout from horizontal to vertical and eliminating the formula layers. All calculations now happen in Looker Studio rather than the spreadsheet. Instead of copying and pasting through complex sheets, staff use clickable filters to target specific data instantly.

The finance team previously spent two hours weekly navigating the labyrinth. That's eliminated. More importantly, non-technical staff can now extract meaningful insights within minutes. The data hasn't changed, but accessibility has—transforming an unusable archive into a working decision-making tool.

Till Data Automation · Multi-million pound charitable enterprise managing Gift Aid tracking, retail EPOS systems, and grant applications across multiple locations
Dec 2025 · Build: 7h · Saves: 11d/yr

Two staff spent an hour weekly extracting sales data from tills and reformatting it for the organisation's master tracking spreadsheet. One managed fourteen shops, the other handled seven. The till system didn't integrate with their existing data structure, which tracked sales alongside Gift Aid and other metrics.

I built automation in Google Sheets using scripts to process the raw exports. The system handles JSON data from the till network and populates the master spreadsheet automatically. For shops in Northern Ireland, the process is now fully automated. Two Scottish locations still require manual entry due to different till systems, taking twenty minutes weekly.

The Northern Ireland manager saves a full hour weekly. The Scotland manager saves forty minutes. That's 100 minutes per week eliminated, recovering eleven working days annually that previously went to copying and pasting numbers between systems.
